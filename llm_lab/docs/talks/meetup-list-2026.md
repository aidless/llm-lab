# Meetup / conference list 2026

> **Goal:** submit to local meetups first (low stakes, friendly
> audience, practice), regional conferences second, national /
> international conferences third.
>
> **Use this list to find CFP deadlines.** Most are 3-6 months
> before the event.

## Tier 1: local meetups (low stakes, practice here)

Submit to **2-3 of these in the same city as you**. Talk length
20-30 min, audience 20-80 people. CFPs are usually rolling or
monthly — email the organizer directly.

## Tier 2: regional conferences (submit 3-6 months early)

These have formal CFPs, 30-40 min talks, 200-1000 attendees. Aim
for 1-2 per year.

- **PyCon DE** — late spring, Germany. https://pycon.de/
- **PyCon CA** — annual, Canada. https://ca.pycon.org/

## Tier 3: ML / AI / security specific (where the wedge is most relevant)

These are the ones most likely to want a talk on "auditable LLM
evaluation". Aim for 1 per year.

- **AI Engineer Summit** — annual, SF/NY. https://www.ai.engineer/
- **LLM Conf / LLM Day** — multiple, dev-focused.
- **ODSC (Open Data Science Conference)** — multiple per year,
  multiple cities.
- **The AI Conference** — annual, US.
- **MLConf** — multiple cities per year. https://mlconf.com/
- **KubeCon** — annual, multiple regions. Has AI/ML co-located
  events. https://www.cncf.io/kubecon/
- **LF AI & Data Day** — co-located with KubeCon, AI/ML focus.
- **SANS AI Security Summit** — annual, US. *Direct match for the
  audit-wedge.*

## How to find new ones

- Search for "[your city] machine learning meetup" and similar.
- Watch the Luma / Meetup "tech events this week" feeds.
- Ask in the r/MachineLearning weekly "what conference should
  I submit to" thread.
- Look at who the *other* OSS projects in your space spoke at
  recently. (`pip` / `ruff` / `pdm` / `uv` / `httpx` maintainers
  often post their talk histories on personal sites.)

## One last rule

**Don't say yes to more than 4 talks per year.** Even if every
conference in the world wants you, every talk costs ~6 hours
(2 prep + 1 practice + 1 deliver + 2 follow-up). 4 talks = 24
hours = a substantial fraction of your OSS time. Less is more.
